Source: Ministry of Textiles, Ministry of MSME, Ministry of Tribal Affairs
Compiled for CraftTrail RAG Knowledge Base

1. PM VISHWAKARMA YOJANA (2023-2028)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Launch date: September 17, 2023 (Vishwakarma Jayanti)
Budget: ₹13,000 crore over 5 years
Implementing ministry: Ministry of MSME

18 eligible crafts/trades:
1. Carpenter (Badhai), 2. Boat Maker, 3. Armourer, 4. Blacksmith (Lohar),
5. Hammer and Tool Kit Maker, 6. Locksmith, 7. Goldsmith (Sonar),
8. Potter (Kumhar), 9. Sculptor (Moortikar, Stone Carver, Stone Breaker),
10. Cobbler/Shoesmith (Mochi, Charmakar), 11. Mason (Rajmistri),
12. Basket/Mat/Broom Maker/Coir Weaver, 13. Doll & Toy Maker (Traditional),
14. Barber (Naai), 15. Garland Maker (Maalakar), 16. Washerman (Dhobi),
17. Tailor (Darzi), 18. Fishing Net Maker

Benefits — Step by step:
STEP 1 — Recognition:
- PM Vishwakarma Certificate + PM Vishwakarma ID Card
- Listed on PM Vishwakarma portal as a recognized craftsperson

STEP 2 — Skill Upgrade:
- 5-7 day basic training at nearest ITI / training centre
- 15-day advanced training for those who qualify
- ₹500 per day stipend during training
- Free training — no fees

STEP 3 — Toolkit Grant:
- ₹15,000 e-voucher to purchase modern tools
- Voucher can be redeemed at authorized tool suppliers
- Tools become property of the artisan

STEP 4 — Credit Support:
- First loan: Up to ₹1,00,000 (₹1 lakh) at 5% interest per annum
- Government pays the difference from market rate (6% interest subvention)
- Second loan: Up to ₹2,00,000 (₹2 lakh) at 5% after repaying first
- No collateral required
- Loan tenure: 18 months (first), 30 months (second)
- Processing through banks: SBI, PNB, Canara Bank, and others

STEP 5 — Digital Transactions:
- ₹1 incentive per digital transaction
- Maximum ₹100/month (100 transactions)
- Total up to ₹1,200/year for using digital payments

STEP 6 — Marketing:
- Access to GeM (Government e-Marketplace) portal
- ONDC (Open Network for Digital Commerce) onboarding
- Participation in trade fairs: Surajkund, IITF, state-level fairs
- E-commerce linkages

How to apply:
1. Visit pmvishwakarma.gov.in
2. Register with Aadhaar and mobile number
3. Village/Urban Level Entrepreneurs (VLEs) at CSC centres help with registration
4. District Implementing Committee verifies application
5. Benefits disbursed in stages after verification
Contact: Toll-free 1800-267-7777

2. NATIONAL HANDICRAFTS DEVELOPMENT PROGRAMME (NHDP)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Implementing agency: Development Commissioner (Handicrafts), Ministry of Textiles
Website: handicrafts.gov.in

Sub-schemes:

A) AMBEDKAR HASTSHILP VIKAS YOJANA (AHVY):
Target: SC/ST artisans, minorities, BPL artisans
Benefits: Skill development, market linkages, common facility centres
Coverage: Rural and urban artisans across India
Eligibility: Must hold Artisan ID Card (Pehchan Card)

B) DESIGN AND TECHNOLOGY UPGRADATION:
- Collaboration with NID (National Institute of Design), Ahmedabad/Bengaluru
- NIFT design intervention for market-relevant product development
- Software tools for design digitization
- Prototyping support

C) MARKETING SUPPORT & SERVICES (MSS):
Events organized:
- Dilli Haat (permanent, New Delhi) — all-year stalls
- Surajkund Mela (February, Haryana) — 30+ day international fair
- International fairs: Cologne (Germany), NY Now (USA), Maison et Objet (France)
- E-commerce on Amazon Karigar, Craftsvilla

D) RESEARCH AND DEVELOPMENT:
- Documentation of endangered crafts
- Craft database: Bhushan (Handicraft Database)
- Research grants to universities and NGOs

E) MEGA CLUSTER DEVELOPMENT:
6 Mega clusters operational:
1. Varanasi (Silk weaving)
2. Mirzapur/Bhadohi (Carpet weaving)
3. Kashmir (Multi-craft)
4. Kutch (Multi-craft — embroidery, tie-dye, printing)
5. Moradabad (Metalwork)
6. West Bengal/Jharkhand border (Dokra/Dhokra)

3. NATIONAL HANDLOOM DEVELOPMENT PROGRAMME (NHDP — WEAVING)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Implementing agency: Office of Development Commissioner for Handlooms
Budget: ₹2,031 crore for 2021-2026

Components:
A) INTEGRATED HANDLOOM CLUSTER DEVELOPMENT SCHEME (IHCDS):
- Cluster approach for handloom weavers
- Common Facility Centres (dyeing, finishing)
- 150+ clusters across India

B) YARN SUPPLY SCHEME:
- Yarn at subsidized rates (10% below market price)
- Through NHDC (National Handloom Development Corporation), Lucknow

C) WEAVERS' SERVICE CENTRES (WSCs):
25 WSCs across India providing:
- Technical assistance on loom setting
- Design services
- Fabric testing
- Quality certification

D) HANDLOOM EXPORT PROMOTION:
- Handloom Export Promotion Council (HEPC)
- Participation in international trade fairs
- Buyer-seller meets

E) e-DHAGA SCHEME:
- Digital identity for weavers
- Online platform for direct selling
- Integrated with GeM portal

4. WEAVERS' WELFARE SCHEMES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

A) MAHATMA GANDHI BUNKAR BIMA YOJANA (MGBBY):
Insurance for handloom weavers:
- Natural death: ₹2,00,000
- Accidental death or total disability: ₹2,00,000
- Partial disability: ₹1,00,000
- Children's scholarship: ₹300/quarter (class 9-12), ₹600/quarter (college)
- Premium: Weaver pays ZERO — fully funded by government
- Implemented through LIC

B) RASHTRIYA SWASTHYA BIMA YOJANA (for weavers below poverty line):
- Health insurance ₹30,000/year per family
- Cashless hospitalization at empanelled hospitals
- Replaced by Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY which covers up to ₹5 lakh

C) WEAVERS' CREDIT CARD (WCC):
- Revolving credit up to ₹2 lakh
- For purchase of: raw material (yarn, dyes), accessories, tools
- Interest rate: Around 7% (with government subvention)
- Issued through nationalized banks

5. ARTISAN CREDIT CARD (ACC)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
For handicraft artisans (not weavers — they have WCC):
- Credit limit: ₹2 lakh (can go higher based on assessment)
- Interest: Market rate minus 6% subvention from government
- No processing fee
- Issued through: SBI, PNB, Bank of Baroda, regional rural banks
- Repayment: Monthly or seasonal (flexible for artisans)
- Renewable annually with satisfactory repayment record

Documents required:
- Artisan ID Card (Pehchan Card)
- Aadhaar Card
- Bank account statement (6 months)
- Workshop address proof
- 2 passport photos

6. MUDRA YOJANA (for artisan micro-enterprises)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Full name: Micro Units Development and Refinance Agency
Website: mudra.org.in

Three tiers:
- SHISHU: Loans up to ₹50,000 — for starting a craft unit
- KISHORE: Loans ₹50,001 to ₹5,00,000 — for expanding
- TARUN: Loans ₹5,00,001 to ₹10,00,000 — for scaling up

Features:
- No collateral required
- MUDRA card (like credit card) for withdrawing sanctioned amount
- Available at: Banks, NBFCs, MFIs, Small Finance Banks
- Special focus on women entrepreneurs (60%+ borrowers are women)

For artisans specifically:
- Craft-specific loans under Shishu for first-time artisan entrepreneurs
- Can be used for: raw materials, tools, renting workshop, working capital

7. TRIFED SCHEMES (FOR TRIBAL ARTISANS)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
TRIFED: Tribal Cooperative Marketing Development Federation of India
Website: trifed.in, tribesindia.com

A) VAN DHAN VIKAS KENDRA (VDVK):
- 3,000+ centres across tribal areas
- Aggregation and value addition of tribal forest produce and crafts
- Helps tribal artisans access markets beyond their village

B) TRIBES INDIA RETAIL NETWORK:
- 130+ Tribes India outlets across India including airports
- Online: tribesindia.com
- Artisans get 70-80% of retail price (vs 20-30% through middlemen)

C) TRIBAL CRAFT MELA:
- Annual crafts fair organized by TRIFED in New Delhi
- Free stall for tribal artisans
- Exposure to domestic and international buyers

D) AAJEEVIKA GRAMEEN EXPRESS YOJANA:
- Transport connectivity for tribal artisans to reach markets

E) EMPOWER SCHEME:
- E-commerce capacity building for tribal artisans
- Onboarding to Amazon, Flipkart, ONDC

9. RECOGNITION AWARDS FOR ARTISANS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

National level (in order of prestige):

1. PADMA SHRI / PADMA BHUSHAN / PADMA VIBHUSHAN:
   Civilian awards from President of India. Many master artisans have received
   Padma Shri — highest for an artisan. Nominees recommended by states, NGOs,
   individuals. Application through awards.gov.in or through state government.

2. SHILP GURU (Master Craftsman Award):
   - Highest recognition from Development Commissioner (Handicrafts)
   - ₹1,00,000 cash prize + certificate + medal
   - Lifetime achievement award for master craftspeople
   - About 20 awarded per year from all of India

3. NATIONAL AWARD FOR MASTER CRAFTSMEN:
   - ₹50,000 cash prize + certificate + medal
   - Given to outstanding craftspeople
   - About 100 awarded per year
   - Priority access to exhibitions and trade fairs after award

4. PRESIDENTIAL AWARD FOR WEAVERS (for handloom weavers):
   - Separate award system for weavers
   - Sant Kabir Award (highest for weavers)
   - National Award for Weavers

5. UNESCO SEAL OF EXCELLENCE FOR HANDICRAFTS:
   - For products meeting UNESCO standards
   - India has many products with this seal
   - Enables export with UNESCO recognition

10. HOW TO REGISTER AS AN ARTISAN — STEP BY STEP
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

PEHCHAN CARD (Artisan Identity Card) — most important first step:

1. Visit nearest Shilpgram or District Industries Centre
2. OR apply online at handicrafts.gov.in
3. Documents needed:
   - Aadhaar Card
   - Photo
   - Bank account details
   - Address proof
   - Craft details (what craft, how long practising)
4. Verification by a registered Verifier or State Craft Officer
5. Card issued within 30-60 days
6. Card valid for 5 years, renewable

UDYAM REGISTRATION (for artisans with small business):
1. Visit udyamregistration.gov.in
2. Self-declaration — no physical documents needed
3. Aadhaar-based registration
4. PAN not mandatory for micro enterprises
5. Benefits: priority bank loans, government tenders, MSME schemes

GEM PORTAL (Government e-Marketplace):
1. Register at gem.gov.in as seller
2. Need: PAN, Aadhaar, bank account, GST (if turnover > ₹20 lakh)
3. List handicraft products for sale to government departments
4. Payments guaranteed within 10 days

11. CHALLENGES FACING INDIAN ARTISANS (AND SOLUTIONS)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

CHALLENGE 1: Competition from machine-made imitations
Solution: GI tags, SILK MARK, HANDLOOM MARK, consumer awareness campaigns.
CraftTrail helps by verifying artisans and connecting consumers directly.

CHALLENGE 2: Low income and irregular earnings
Solution: PM Vishwakarma scheme, Artisan Credit Card, cooperative marketing,
e-commerce platforms, CraftTrail workshop booking (direct income to artisan).

CHALLENGE 3: No next generation taking up the craft
Solution: NHDP skill development, academic partnerships (craft schools),
NID/NIFT design interventions, international recognition increasing prestige.

CHALLENGE 4: Raw material access and quality
Solution: Government yarn supply schemes, raw material banks at cluster level,
Common Facility Centres with testing and processing equipment.

CHALLENGE 5: Market access — stuck with middlemen
Solution: Dilli Haat, GeM portal, ONDC, Tribes India, CraftTrail direct booking,
Amazon Karigar, Flipkart Samarth, TRIFED.

CHALLENGE 6: No documentation of traditional knowledge
Solution: Craft documentation projects by Crafts Council of India, state governments,
university research, UNESCO intangible cultural heritage nominations.

CHALLENGE 7: Climate change affecting natural materials
Solution: Sustainable forestry practices, government-regulated raw material
sourcing, organic certification programs for natural dye artisans.
